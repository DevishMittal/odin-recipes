Rajma Chawal

Description

Rajma chawal is a popular dish in Indian cuisine that consists of kidney beans cooked in a tomato and onion-based gravy, served with steamed rice. It is a staple in many households and is enjoyed for its hearty and comforting flavors. The dish is often seasoned with a blend of aromatic spices such as cumin, coriander, and garam masala, which gives it a rich and flavorful taste. Rajma chawal is also a nutritious meal, as kidney beans are a good source of protein and fiber, while rice provides carbohydrates. It is a filling and satisfying dish that can be enjoyed for lunch or dinner, and is often paired with a side of pickles, yogurt, or papadums.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup rajma (kidney beans)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala powder
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder (optional)
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic, finely chopped
  • 1 inch ginger, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • Salt to taste
  • 3 cups water
  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 2 cups water (for cooking rice)
  • 1 tbsp ghee (optional)
  • Fresh coriander leaves, finely chopped (for garnishing)

Steps

  1. Soak 1 cup of rajma (kidney beans) in water overnight or for at least 8 hours.
  2. Drain the water from the soaked rajma and rinse them well.
  3. In a pressure cooker, heat 2 tbsp of oil and add 1 tsp cumin seeds.
  4. When the cumin seeds start to splutter, add 1 finely chopped onion and sauté until golden brown.
  5. Add 2-3 cloves of chopped garlic and 1 inch of chopped ginger. Sauté for a minute.
  6. Add 2 finely chopped tomatoes and sauté until they turn mushy.
  7. Add 1 tsp coriander powder, 1 tsp garam masala powder, 1 tsp turmeric powder, and 1 tsp red chili powder (optional). Mix well.
  8. Add the soaked and rinsed rajma to the cooker along with 3 cups of water and salt to taste.
  9. Pressure cook the rajma for about 10-12 whistles or until they are soft and fully cooked.
  10. In a separate pot, wash 1 cup of basmati rice and add 2 cups of water.
  11. Cook the rice until it is fully cooked and fluffy.
  12. Once the rajma is fully cooked, open the cooker and check if the gravy has a medium consistency. If it's too thin, simmer it for a few more minutes until it thickens.
  13. Turn off the heat and add 1 tbsp of ghee (optional) to the rajma and mix well.
  14. Serve the rajma hot with the steamed rice and garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
